# IFRS 8 Operating Segments — Research for Department Accounting in Vietnamese SMEs

**Researched:** 2026-07-21

---

## 1. IFRS 8 Status (as of 2026)

**Active.** Not superseded. IFRS 8 *Operating Segments* is part of the 2026 Issued Standards (amendments up to 31 Dec 2025). Originally issued November 2006, replacing IAS 14 *Segment Reporting*. Effective for periods beginning on or after 1 Jan 2009.

Recent amendments:
- **IFRS 18** (issued Apr 2024, effective date pending) amended paragraph 23 — references to IAS 1 replaced with IFRS 18.
- **IFRS Interpretations Committee** (Jul 2024, updated Jan 2026): Agenda decision on *Disclosure of Revenues and Expenses for Reportable Segments* — confirmed entities must disclose specified amounts (para 23(a)–(i)) if they are included in segment P&L reviewed by CODM *or* regularly provided to CODM, even if not included in the measure.

---

## 2. IFRS 8 — Management Approach

**Core principle (para 1):** Disclose information to enable users to evaluate the nature and financial effects of the different business activities and economic environments in which the entity operates.

**"Management approach":** Segments are identified based on the internal reports that the chief operating decision maker (CODM) uses to allocate resources and assess performance. This is the key departure from IAS 14 (and VAS 28), which used a risk-and-rewards approach with rigid business/geographic split.

**CODM definition (para 7):** A function, not a title — the function of allocating resources and assessing performance. Often CEO or COO, but could be a group of executive directors.

**No "primary/secondary" segmentation** — IFRS 8 dropped the IAS 14 primary/secondary segment distinction. The CODM's internal view determines the single set of reportable segments.

---

## 3. Operating Segment Definition (para 5)

Three characteristics — a component of an entity:

| # | Characteristic |
|---|----------------|
| a | Engages in business activities, earns revenues and incurs expenses (including inter-segment transactions) |
| b | Operating results regularly reviewed by CODM to allocate resources and assess performance |
| c | Discrete financial information available |

**Not every part is an operating segment** — corporate HQ, functional departments with only incidental revenue, post-employment benefit plans.

**Segment manager (para 9):** Function directly accountable to CODM. One manager may serve multiple segments.

**Matrix organisations (para 10):** Where overlapping sets exist (e.g., product-line × geography), CODM determines which set constitutes operating segments by reference to the core principle.

---

## 4. Reportable Segment Tests (paras 11–15)

A reportable segment = an operating segment (or aggregation thereof) that meets quantitative thresholds.

### 10% Quantitative Thresholds (para 13)

Any **one** of:

| Test | Threshold |
|------|-----------|
| Revenue (external + intersegment) | ≥ 10% of combined revenue of all operating segments |
| Profit/Loss (absolute) | ≥ 10% of the greater of (i) combined profit of profitable segments, (ii) combined loss of loss-making segments |
| Assets | ≥ 10% of combined assets of all operating segments |

### 75% "Coverage Test" (para 15)

If reportable segments' external revenue < 75% of total entity revenue, **additional segments** must be identified as reportable (even if below 10%) until ≥ 75% is covered.

### Other rules
- **Below-threshold segments** may be voluntarily disclosed if management believes useful.
- **Below-threshold aggregation** permitted only if segments have similar economic characteristics and share a majority of aggregation criteria (para 14).
- **Prior-period carry-forward:** If an operating segment was reportable in the immediately preceding period and is judged of continuing significance, continue reporting it separately (para 16).
- **Practical limit:** As segments exceed ~10, consider whether detail becomes excessive (para 19).

---

## 5. Aggregation Criteria (para 12)

Two or more operating segments may be aggregated into one **only if** all three conditions met:
1. Aggregation consistent with core principle.
2. Segments have **similar economic characteristics** (e.g., similar long-term average gross margins).
3. Segments are similar in **each** of the following respects:

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| a | Nature of products and services |
| b | Nature of production processes |
| c | Type/class of customer for products and services |
| d | Methods used to distribute products or provide services |
| e | Nature of regulatory environment (if applicable, e.g., banking, insurance, utilities) |

**Judgements must be disclosed** (para 22(aa)) — economic indicators assessed, brief description of aggregated segments.

---

## 6. Entity-Wide Disclosures (paras 31–34)

Apply to **all entities within scope** (including those with a single reportable segment). Provided **only if not already given** via segment disclosures.

### Products and Services (para 32)
Revenues from external customers for each product/service or each group of similar products/services.

### Geographical Areas (para 33)
| Item | Disclosure |
|------|------------|
| Revenues from external customers | (i) Country of domicile, (ii) all foreign countries in total. Material individual foreign countries disclosed separately. |
| Non-current assets (excl. financial instruments, deferred tax, post-employment benefits, insurance rights) | (i) Country of domicile, (ii) all foreign countries in total. Material individual countries disclosed separately. |

Disclose basis for attributing revenues to countries.

### Major Customers (para 34)
If revenues from a **single external customer** ≥ 10% of entity's revenues:
- Disclose that fact.
- Total amount of revenues from each such customer.
- Identity of segment(s) reporting the revenues.
- (Customer identity not required, but often disclosed in practice.)

"Single customer" includes a group under common control.

### Cost-to-develop exemption
Entity-wide disclosure not required if "necessary information is not available and the cost to develop it would be excessive" (paras 33, 34). Rarely invoked — information is entity-wide, not segment-level.

---

## 7. Scope — Who Must Comply

IFRS 8 applies to:
- Entities whose **debt or equity securities are publicly traded** (listed).
- Entities **in the process of issuing securities** in public markets.
- If consolidated FS are prepared, segment disclosures are based on the consolidated group structure.

**Not required for private entities / SMEs** — but may be voluntarily applied.

## 8. VAS 28 — Vietnamese Segment Reporting Standard

### Status
- **VAS 28** (Chuẩn mực kế toán Việt Nam số 28 — Báo cáo bộ phận) is **active**.
- Issued: 15 Feb 2005 (Quyết định 12/2005/QĐ-BTC), effective 23 Mar 2005.
- **Based on IAS 14 (old)** — not IFRS 8. Has not been updated to reflect the management approach.

### Key Features of VAS 28
- **Risk-and-rewards approach** (not management approach).
- **Primary/secondary segment** structure: entity determines whether business segments or geographic segments are primary; the other is secondary.
- **10% quantitative thresholds** (same as IFRS 8 for revenue, profit/loss, assets).
- **75% coverage rule**.
- **Aggregation criteria** similar to IFRS 8.
- **Scope:** Public companies and enterprises in process of issuing securities.

### Scope Details
VAS 28 applies to enterprises whose equity or debt securities are publicly traded and those in the process of public issuance. The standard encourages (but does not require) segment reporting for integrated production processes.

### VAS 28 vs. IFRS 8 — Key Differences

| Aspect | IFRS 8 | VAS 28 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Approach | Management approach (CODM view) | Risk-and-rewards approach |
| Segment structure | Single set of operating segments | Primary + secondary segments |
| Basis of segmentation | Internal management reporting | Business activity × geographic area |
| CODM concept | Central concept | Not defined (uses "Board of Directors" / management) |
| Entity-wide disclosures | Products/services, geography, major customers | Secondary segment disclosures |
| Underlying standard | SFAS 131 (US GAAP) → IFRS 8 | IAS 14 (old) |
| Year issued | 2006 (effective 2009) | 2005 |

## 9. Segment Reporting for Vietnamese SMEs

### Current Position
- **VAS 28** applies to **public companies only** — SMEs not required.
- **Thông tư 133/2016/TT-BTC** (SME accounting regime) specifies financial statement templates for SMEs. These templates do **not** include mandatory segment reporting.
- **Thông tư 200/2014/TT-BTC** (general enterprise accounting regime, replaced by TT 99/2025) similarly did not require segment reporting.

### Bottom line for SMEs
**No mandatory segment reporting under VAS or Vietnamese accounting regime.** IFRS 8 does not apply unless the SME voluntarily adopts IFRS (only large enterprises / public interest entities typically do this in Vietnam).

### Relevance to SME accounting software
Segment reporting is **voluntary / management-driven** for SMEs. If directors want internal management accounts broken down by department, the system can support it — but no regulatory requirement.

---

## 10. TT 99/2025/TT-BTC and Segment Reporting

### What TT 99/2025 does
- Replaces TT 200/2014 from **1 Jan 2026**.
- Renames "Bảng cân đối kế toán" → "Báo cáo tình hình tài chính".
- Updates financial statement templates (B01-DN, B02-DN, B03-DN, B09-DN).
- Adds separate templates for entities no longer a going concern (B01-DNKLT, etc.).
- Allows enterprises to add supplementary line items for management purposes (with internal regulations).

### What TT 99/2025 does NOT do
- **No mandatory segment reporting** (báo cáo bộ phận) required.
- Financial statement templates follow the same structure as TT 200/2014 — no segment breakdown.
- Maintains reference to VAS 28 for segment reporting (if applicable), but does not expand its scope.

### Implications
TT 99/2025 modernises presentation and disclosure but leaves segment reporting standards unchanged. Vietnamese SMEs still face **no legal obligation** to produce segment disclosures.

## 11. Relevance to This Project (Department Accounting)

| Aspect | Relevance |
|--------|-----------|
| **Department = operating segment?** | IFRS 8 defines operating segments by CODM review — internal departments could qualify if CODM reviews their results. |
| **Management approach useful?** | The IFRS 8 management approach aligns naturally with department-based accounting — if managers review department P&Ls, those departments are "operating segments." |
| **Not required for SMEs** | No regulatory compulsion. But software could optionally produce segment-style reports. |
| **VAS 28 gap** | VAS 28 is outdated (IAS 14-based). Vietnam's roadmap to IFRS (Decision 345/QD-BTC 2020) contemplates IFRS adoption for listed entities first; SMEs remain on VAS. |
| **TT 99/2025** | No new segment reporting mandates. |

### Recommendation
- Implement **optional** department-based segment reporting in the software.
- Model after IFRS 8's management approach (CODM-driven) — more intuitive than VAS 28's primary/secondary structure.
- Allow user to configure which departments are "operating segments" and what measures (revenue, expenses, assets) are tracked.
- Provide entity-wide disclosures (major customer concentration, product/service categories) as optional modules.
